History

The Lamborghini Huracán made its debut in 2014, replacing the iconic Gallardo as Lamborghini's entry-level supercar. Named after a Spanish fighting bull, the Huracán blends cutting-edge technology with raw power. Powered by a 5.2-liter V10 engine, it quickly became known for its stunning performance, reaching speeds of over 200 mph. Over the years, Lamborghini has introduced various versions, including the Performante and Evo, each pushing the boundaries of design and driving dynamics, solidifying the Huracán as a symbol of automotive excellence.

Features

The Lamborghini Huracán combines a 5.2-liter V10 engine with up to 640 horsepower, delivering thrilling speed and precision handling. It features advanced all-wheel or rear-wheel drive, dynamic steering, and carbon-ceramic brakes for exceptional performance. Inside, a luxurious cockpit with cutting-edge tech and customizable driving modes offers a personalized driving experience. The Huracán is the ultimate fusion of power, agility, and luxury.
